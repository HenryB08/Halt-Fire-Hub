The Science

How HALT Industrial Eliminates
All Three Legs of the Fire Triangle.

Traditional foams only slow the spread. HALT Industrial simultaneously removes heat, fuel, AND oxygen — making re-ignition impossible.

HEAT

Instant Cooling

GreenFire® technology overcomes the Leidenfrost effect — the steam barrier that prevents water from contacting surfaces above 2,000°F. Food-grade wetting agents allow direct molecular contact with the hottest surfaces, removing heat faster than any competing product.

OXYGEN

Foam Smothering

A stable foam blanket seals the fuel surface completely — cutting off oxygen and suppressing vapors. Unlike water, which runs off, the foam maintains coverage, preventing the fire from accessing air until the fuel is inert. No oxygen = no fire. No re-ignition.

FUEL

Boron Retardant

Natural boron compounds penetrate the fuel surface and promote char formation — making the material non-combustible. Free radicals that propagate oxidation are sequestered. The retardant layer persists after extinguishment, preventing re-ignition for days without re-application.

Formula:
Natural boron compounds (glassy boron agents) · Food-grade wetting agents · Anionic detergents · Proprietary mineral blend (29 CFR 1910.1200). Zero PFAS · Zero fluorine · Zero VOCs · Zero Prop 65 components.

The PFAS Crisis

Why AFFF Has To Go.

Conventional firefighting foams contain PFAS — man-made "forever chemicals" that do not break down in the environment or the human body. They bioaccumulate over decades and have contaminated groundwater at hundreds of military bases, airports, and fire training sites across the USA.

Cancer is now the leading cause of death among firefighters. PFAS exposure has been linked to: testicular cancer, kidney cancer, liver cancer, pancreatic cancer, thyroid disease, and immune system dysfunction.

"Firefighters run a considerable risk of cancer when exposed to PFAS chemicals. Once dispersed, these chemicals reside in the body for years — going undetected while accumulating to dangerous levels."

Firefighting Foam (AFFF)

Major source of groundwater contamination at military bases, airports, and fire training facilities. Hundreds of contaminated sites — millions of affected Americans.

Firefighter Gear & Equipment

Turnout gear, coats, and gloves contain PFAS for water resistance. PFAS leaches from gear through skin contact and inhalation during every fire call.

Drinking Water Contamination

PFAS contaminate groundwater, rivers, and streams near facilities. Ammonia and heavy metals in traditional retardants trigger algal blooms — making water unsafe.

The DOD has already spent $2.6B investigating PFAS groundwater contamination. Every fire department faces growing legal liability for AFFF use.

What's Inside

The Formula That Changed Everything.

HALT fire protection technology is built on a proprietary blend of naturally occurring chemical compounds and minerals — zero synthetic PFAS, zero fluorine, zero VOCs, zero Prop 65 components.

What they created in 2016 was something never before seen in fire protection: a highly effective wetting agent made from environmentally friendly materials that was non-corrosive, PFAS-free, non-carcinogenic, and certified food-safe. It immediately won the NSF International Registration Mark — the first time in history that certification was ever given to a fire protection product.

Natural Boron Compounds

Glassy boron agents penetrate cellulose materials and promote char formation — making the fuel surface itself non-combustible. Retardant minerals persist indefinitely unless leached by ¼" or more of rainfall.

Food Grade Wetting Agents

Biodegradable surfactants that overcome the Leidenfrost effect — the steam barrier that prevents water from contacting hot surfaces. Allows direct contact with surfaces exceeding 2,000°F for immediate cooling.

Anionic Detergents

Creates a stable foam blanket covering the fuel surface — cutting off oxygen supply and suppressing vapors. Behaves as a shear-thinning non-Newtonian fluid: flows like water through hoses under pressure, thickens to maple-syrup consistency on contact with the target surface for enhanced coverage.

Fire Triangle — All Three Legs Eliminated
Heat

Wetting agents provide immediate cooling to even the hottest surfaces — extinguishing time reduced, fewer firefighters and less water needed.

Oxygen

Foam blanket seals the fuel surface, preventing oxygen from reaching the fire and vapors from leaving the fuel — the fire cannot continue without both.

Fuel

Boron retardants sequester free radicals that propagate oxidation reactions, and promote surface char — rendering the fuel non-combustible and preventing re-ignition.

Why It Matters

PFAS Are Forever Chemicals.

Where PFAS Comes From

AFFF firefighting foam — major source of groundwater contamination at airports and military bases where firefighting training occurs.

Consumer products — Teflon, stain-resistant fabrics, waxes, paints, cleaning products. PFAS are in virtually every American home.

Drinking water — localized contamination near manufacturers, landfills, wastewater plants, and fire training facilities.

Living organisms — PFAS bioaccumulate in fish, wildlife, and humans. They cannot be broken down by the body — building up over a lifetime.

Cancer is now the #1 cause of death among firefighters. PFAS exposure has been linked to each of these conditions in epidemiological studies. Unlike other toxins, PFAS cannot be metabolized — they remain in the bloodstream indefinitely, accumulating with every exposure.

HALT contains zero PFAS, zero fluorine, zero VOCs, and no California Prop 65 components. It is certified by NSF as food-safe — meaning HALT is so clean it can be used safely around food preparation areas.

Protecting Waterways

Zero Phosphorus.
Zero Contamination.

Traditional fire retardants contain ammonium phosphate, heavy metals (chromium, cadmium, vanadium), and other toxins that trigger algal blooms, kill fish, and make water unsafe for drinking.

HALT Industrial products contain zero phosphorus — eliminating the major driver of nutrient pollution in waterways. In all categories of aquatic toxicity testing, HALT Wetting Agent scored as EPA "Practically Non-Toxic" — the best achievable rating — posing minimal risk to fish, amphibians, and aquatic life.

HALT products have been approved for use in Bambi Bucket helicopter operations — allowing pilots to mix wetting agent or foam into water before drops, significantly increasing firefighting effectiveness while protecting the rivers, lakes, and streams below.
