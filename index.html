The Direct Link Between AFFF and Cancer

AFFF contains PFOS and PFOA — classified by the EPA as hazardous substances under CERCLA since April 2024. These fluorosurfactants bioaccumulate in the body and never break down in soil or groundwater. The DOD has already spent $2.6 billion investigating PFAS groundwater contamination at military bases alone — and is required by federal law to eliminate AFFF from all installations by October 2026.

The NIOSH Firefighter Cancer Study — tracking 30,000 career firefighters across three major US cities — confirmed significantly elevated cancer risk across multiple types. The firefighting occupation was reclassified by IARC to Group 1 carcinogenic in 2022. With 3M settling AFFF contamination claims for $10.3 billion and 34+ states introducing bans, the transition away from AFFF is no longer a question of if — it's a question of what comes next. How to Use HALT

No training. No PIN to pull. No annual inspection. Any member of your household can use HALT safely and effectively in seconds — the first time, every time.

1

Assess the Situation

Evaluate the type and size of the fire. HALT is designed for small, early-stage fires. If the fire is large or spreading rapidly, evacuate immediately and call 911.

2

Aim at the Base

Point the nozzle at the base of the fire — where fuel meets flame. HALT's formula penetrates and coats the fuel source to cut off the oxygen supply at its source.

3

Squeeze & Sweep

Squeeze the handle to release the extinguishing agent. Sweep side to side until the fire is out. HALT's persistent coating prevents reignition for lasting protection.

Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

What types of fires does HALT work on?
+
HALT is effective on Class A (wood, paper, cloth), Class B (gasoline, oils, flammable liquids), Class K (cooking oils and grease), and lithium-ion battery fires. HALT Li-Ion is specifically engineered to stop thermal runaway in battery fires — the only consumer product of its kind.
Is HALT safe around food and children?
+
Yes. HALT holds NSF Registration and USDA certification since 2016. It is non-toxic, non-irritating, and leaves no harmful residue. A quick rinse with water is all the cleanup required after use on food surfaces or food-contact equipment.
How long does HALT last on the shelf?
+
HALT has a 4-year shelf life and requires zero maintenance or inspections. Traditional fire extinguishers require annual professional inspections and can cost $100+ per service visit. Simply check the expiration date printed on the HALT can.
What certifications does HALT hold?
+
HALT holds: NSF & USDA (food-safe since 2016), SwRI (wetting agent performance testing), NFPA 18 compliance, UL 711 (Underwriters Laboratories), GreenScreen (environmental safety), OMRI (organic farming environments), and DOD/DOE/EPA/FAA industrial testing for GFFF.
What is PFAS and why does it matter?
+
PFAS are "per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances" — synthetic chemicals called "forever chemicals" because they never break down in the environment or the human body. Traditional fire foams containing PFAS are linked to cancer, reproductive harm, and permanent groundwater contamination. HALT achieves equal or superior fire suppression using biodegradable, PFAS-free surfactants.
Where is HALT made?
+
HALT Fire products are made in the United States by Prime Safety US Inc. Built on GreenFire®'s patented formula established in 2016 — now expanding nationally under the HALT brand with three product divisions.

The HALT Science — Patented GreenFire® Technology

How HALT Works

HALT's patented GreenFire® formula attacks fire at the molecular level — breaking the fire triangle through advanced wetting, thermal absorption, and persistent vapor barrier technology. 100% PFAS-free, fluorine-free, and phosphorous-free. Peer-reviewed, independently tested, and certified by NSF, UL, GreenScreen, and OMRI.

Fire Triangle Science

Three Elements. One Solution.

Every fire requires three elements: fuel, heat, and oxygen. HALT's patented formula attacks all three simultaneously — extinguishing faster and preventing reignition longer than any PFAS-based alternative.

Fuel

HALT's unique surfactant chemistry penetrates porous fuel sources — wood, fabric, cardboard, insulation — coating and sealing the combustible surface to prevent fuel vapor release.

HALT Penetrates & Seals

Heat

HALT's aqueous base carries significant heat capacity — rapidly cooling combustion materials to below ignition temperature. The persistent barrier continues absorbing heat after application.

HALT Cools & Absorbs

Oxygen

By forming an impenetrable surface coating that does not evaporate, HALT cuts off the oxygen supply to the fuel source — starving the fire and preventing reignition for hours after application.

HALT Seals & Starves
The Chemistry Behind HALT

Patented Formula. Proven Performance.

HALT achieves fire suppression through three parallel mechanisms — each addressing a different aspect of the combustion chain reaction.

Surfactant Chemistry

HALT uses biodegradable, fluorine-free surfactants that dramatically reduce the surface tension of water — enabling superior penetration into porous fuel sources at a fraction of the concentration of traditional wetting agents.

Thermal Absorption

The high specific heat capacity of HALT's aqueous base rapidly absorbs thermal energy from combustion, cooling materials below ignition temperature within seconds of application — faster than any PFAS-based alternative in independent testing.

Persistent Barrier

Unlike water or legacy foams that evaporate, HALT's formula absorbs into material fibers and surfaces. The protective barrier does not evaporate — providing lasting protection against reignition for hours after the initial extinguishment.

PFAS-Free Surfactants

Traditional AFFF uses fluorosurfactants (PFAS) that bioaccumulate and persist in the environment for thousands of years. HALT replaces these with biodegradable surfactants that achieve equivalent or superior fire suppression without any persistent environmental impact.

Our History

The Road to HALT

From founding to the present day — a history of pushing the boundaries of what fire suppression technology can be.

2016

GreenFire Founded

GreenFire® is formed to provide a safe & effective green fire suppression alternative — establishing the patented PFAS-free formula and science that would power the entire HALT product line.

2018

First NSF Registration for Food-Safe Fire Protection

GreenFire® obtains the first NSF Registration Mark for a food-safe fire protection product as a wetting agent — confirming the formula is safe around food processing equipment and food preparation surfaces.

2020

Multi-Class Patent Secured

GreenFire® obtains a Multi-Class A/B Wetting Agent Firefighting Foam patent — cementing the proprietary PFAS-free formula as the leading technology for structure, wildland, and vehicle fire suppression.

2022

UL Certification & ASTM E84 Ratings

GreenFire® obtains Multi-Class Foam Wetting Agent UL Certification. GreenFire Pro Defense obtains ASTM E84 rating. GreenFire® also obtains ASTM E84 Class 1(A) rating — meeting the highest standards in fire safety and building materials.

2024

HALT! Consumer Products Formed

HALT! Fire consumer products are formed using the GreenFire® formula — bringing proven PFAS-free fire suppression technology into the home as a safer & more effective solution for everyday consumers.

2025

HALT! Industrial Formed & National Consumer Expansion

HALT! Industrial is officially formed — combining HALT!'s sales and commercial operations with the science and expertise of the GreenFire® formula. HALT! Fire home extinguishers and fire blankets expand nationwide across grocery, military, drug, and Amazon channels simultaneously.

2026

HALT Fire Acquires GreenFire® — One Unified Platform

In April 2026, HALT Fire (Prime Safety US Inc.) officially acquires Fire Suppression Innovations Inc. (GreenFire®), announced via PR Newswire. The acquisition creates America's most complete PFAS-free fire suppression platform — unifying consumer, industrial, and professional divisions under a single brand committed to zero forever chemicals.
